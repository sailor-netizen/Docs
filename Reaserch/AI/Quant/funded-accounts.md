## Why Traditional Prop Firms Are Considered Inferior

- Traders trade on **simulated accounts** — not real capital
- **Profit splits** eat realized gains
- Evaluation fees: typically ~$250 upfront
- Arbitrary rule sets designed to maximize failure rate (drawdown limits, consistency rules, time windows)
- No alignment between firm and trader

## Scaling Algorithm

Three factors determine funding increases:
1. **Trading consistency** — not just P&L; surviving and trading regularly
2. **Streaming engagement** — viewership, interaction
3. **Trading performance** — win rate, drawdown management

First-come, first-served on acceptance. Throttling applied due to high demand volume.

## The "Bumpers" Concept

Quant App is described as bumpers on a bowling lane — the trader keeps their own style and strategy but risk controls are enforced by the system rather than relying on willpower:

- Visible liquidation proximity for all Hyperliquid positions (can see other traders' distance from liquidation)
- Position-sizing discipline enforced by app UI
- Entry based on liquidation data (entering near others' liquidation prices as a contrarian signal)
- Trade logging for review and strategy iteration

**Stated rationale for 40x leverage despite risk:** Using bumpers (the Quant App risk controls) extends trader lifespan on high-leverage accounts significantly. Four-week live test prior to launch showed profitability — or at minimum, survival — that would not have been achieved without the system.
